Avenue Night Market - Phan Thong Details

\

The Avenue Night Market, also known as Grand Market Avenue @ Map Pong, is strategically located next to the Amata Phan Thong Industrial Estate (part of Amata City Chonburi) in Chonburi Province, Thailand. This expansive industrial estate spans thousands of hectares and hosts over 700 companies, employing more than 200,000 workers, driving significant local economic activity. The market leverages this industrial hub's growth, with investments exceeding 50,000 baht per project and a robust 15% annual land sales growth, fueled by the Eastern Economic Corridor (EEC) development. Operating daily, the 500-stall market benefits from high foot traffic from industrial workers, residents, and visitors, ensuring consistent revenue from stall rentals and events.

\

Key Features:

    • \
  • Garden spaces (green) and restrooms (light green) for visitor comfort.
    • \
  • A central stage area for events, entertainment, and community gatherings, enhancing appeal.
    • \
  • Proximity to major infrastructure, supporting logistics and accessibility.
    • \
\

Funding Needs: This project requires investment for development, operations, and expansion to meet the growing demand in the Phan Thong area, where land prices remain stable but show potential for appreciation due to EEC initiatives. Beach Night Market - Jomtien Details

\

The Beach Night Market in Jomtien is a premier beachside destination in Pattaya, Chonburi Province, Thailand. Situated along Jomtien Beach Road facing the sea, it capitalizes on Pattaya's thriving tourism industry, which saw hotel occupancy rates exceeding 70% in 2024 and continued growth in 2025. With average Airbnb revenues around $10,269 per year per property and a 61% occupancy rate (higher than the city average of 34-47%), the area attracts millions of visitors annually for its beaches, nightlife, and entertainment. The market operates in the evenings, aligning with peak tourist hours, offering a vibrant mix of local culture, food, and shopping to generate steady rental income.

\

Key Features:

\
    \
  • Diverse stalls featuring fresh seafood, grilled fish, sushi, chicken, juices, and other Thai street foods at affordable prices.
    • \
  • Souvenirs, clothing, and knick-knacks for tourists, with some halal options available.
    • \
  • Bars and lively atmosphere with music, sea views, and a continuous flow of visitors.
    • \
  • Proximity to the beach for scenic evening strolls and events.
    • \
  • Convenient location near hotels and attractions, drawing both locals and international tourists.
    • \
\

Funding Needs: Investment is needed for beachfront setup, marketing, and infrastructure to tap into Pattaya's tourism surge, where short-term rental revenues average $8,000-$17,000 annually per host. Shares are available at 200,000 baht per 1%, with a minimum investment of 1% and a maximum of 49% ownership per person.
